Why? Answers from an industry in the sunset

When the clock is turned 10 years, 20 years back into the initial era of world wide web, or even longer into the Web 1.0 age consisting of only simple systems like mailing, Fidonet and Gopher. These days, hackers trived from the transition era where many things were not completely understood and many logical errors, unthought vectors and other reasons that led to hacking become a common game for anyone with a creative mindset in the technical industries.

By the time Amiga’s race with Intel lost in 1995s, and Intel officially rised into power with their complete dominance over computer processor industry, vulnerabilities arised from hardware level issues have already started to decrease. Forwarding the clock into post world wide web era, the term “zero day” becomes more and more known for a vulnerability that are undisclosed. Zero day vulnerability are defined as a compromisation vector that are not yet publicized and most likely not known by others, such pieces of knowledge can be reported, or sold for money.

The concept of zero day and script kiddies had always been muddled and confused due to various misleading publications, both are in fact the same other than the process of it.

To acquire a zero day, other than discovering it through professional knowledge of coding and the targeted platform, module, or hardware. It can be purchased, from black markets or from security firms that trade zero days vulnerabilities. There are many such information on the Internet such as Wikileaks, thus this post will not further venture into the topic. 

What the explanation intended, was for the understanding that anyone can have the powers of using zero days against widely popular applications or platforms by knowing the right people, basic knowledge of computing to understand how to use the information or maybe a provided proof of concept code. 

Another most important factor, is the price of it. When the trading of zero days initially started maybe twenty years ago, they were cheap and carelessly sold and abused for fame, anywhere from a couple hundred dollars you can obtain something that might be capable to cause mass destruction. Currently in 2022, any vulnerabilities that are possible to be used for wide survilliance, damages, or other profitable hacking purposes are either at least in the 6-figures price range, or priceless and not traded easily.
